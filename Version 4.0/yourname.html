The storytelling within "Your Name." , after taking a step back, opened my eyes to the magnificent elegance it portrayed to viewers like me. To know that stories like these exist really astound me to this day and helped to spark my interest in writing once more. Heck, it even urged me to write a review for it, despite me almost never writing reviews for shows or movies, even if they were my favorites/ones I loved. While the plot had its ups and downs (just little nitpicks and MAINLY UPS) here and there, the music, animation, and the mere execution of the movie, in my opinion, makes up for it in its entirety. Despite its supernatural elements, "Your Name." exhibited a large amount of humanity and the interactions and thoughts between characters felt real, given the sentiment and each individual's longing, relishing us with a sense of genuineness and realism amidst the otherworldly plot. The animation is the best I've seen as of my rookie career as an anime enjoyer so I don't really have much expertise to say here but I can assure that the graphics goes above and beyond than even what some Japanese animation films provide today. The music also tugged SEVERAL chords in my heart and to be frank, the ending always gets me because of it's music choice and orchestration. Yet, they are many more songs of significance like "Zenzenzense" or "Sparkle" to be mentioned. Though, you should hear them yourself if you ever watch the movie. It's been literally 2 days since I watched the movie and I already re-watched it with a close friend today and I plan to re-watch it even more in the future as more people who are unfamiliar with the movie comes along. I urge that everyone should at least watch this movie once, both for the story and the mere experience of watching of what I think of, is a masterpiece in today's world of film animation.

4 years on, and this movie is still wedged in my heart. I see reviews here trying to describe this movie. A romcom about two teenagers and an interesting ability to swap bodies at any time within a week. They both grow to love each other as they live in the other's lives. However, there is nothing I could do to replicate or try to explain what I really experienced out of the 6 times I've watched this film. The artwork is without a doubt stunning, the prettiest animation I've seen across any medium. The music is heartfelt, melancholic, inscribed into my heart and brain. I could skip to any part of the movie and hum the OST accurately. Not to mention the 4 incredibly moving and addictive songs - Yumetouru, Zen Zen Zense, Sparkle and Nandemonaiya. Each of these 4 have their own unique sound, with beautifully written lyrics that paint a fantastical picture in my mind every time I listen to them. The movie's theme of destiny, fate, and longing for someone may not be new, but it was executed perfectly. Its atmosphere and mood placed me in a space where I didn't know existed. Everything felt so dreamy, out of this world, so astronomical, yet, so attainable. But, like I said. I could never replicate and regurgitate my feelings, my experience, when I watched Taki and Mitsuha struggling beautifully in this film. So, go and watch it. 10/10, my all-time favourite movie.
