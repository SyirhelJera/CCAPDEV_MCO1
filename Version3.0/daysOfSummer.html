This film hits especially close to home for anyone who has ever punched above their weight or wrongly idolised someone they were physically attracted to. I believe that the protagonist's experience is no better summed up than in the 'expectations vs reality' scene. This scene is one of the most masterfully written, acted and directed scenes I have ever seen. It hits so close to home with so many viewers. Above all else, this movie eloquently takes us on the journey of 'boy meets girl' without the happy ending of 'boy marries girl.' And (*SPOILER*) while the ending of 'boy meets even hotter girl and presumably lives happily ever after with her' is a little farfetched, it does accurately reflect the fact that most people in the protagonist's position do move on eventually and find someone new - someone more appropriate and 'real'. Story: 4/5. It's charming and funny, as anything with Zooey often is. The film outlines what it's about early on, making it clear that it really is about the experience more than it is about the journey. It's not a ground-breaking journey by any stretch, but it is a fun and sometimes tumultuous one. The cast was all-round pretty good. Visuals: 3/5. The cinematography as a whole isn't anything more than standard, but there are sequences here and there that explored some interesting ideas and framing. The rooftop party scene, to be specific. Sound: 4/5. The soundtrack is spectacular. The person behind the music choices did a great job and had (at least, in my eyes) really good taste. The use of the music was appropriate without ever being awkwardly brief or overstaying its welcome. 11/15: A good time with a couple of laughs and a touch of introspection.<3
